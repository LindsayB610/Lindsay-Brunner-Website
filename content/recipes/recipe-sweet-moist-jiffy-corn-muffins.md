I guess I've been on a carb kick... Honestly, when am I not on a carb kick? I've been making beer bread almost weekly, and decided this week to try something new. Enter these Jiffy corn muffins. I ADORE cornbread, but it always bums me out with its dry, crumbly texture when what I want is the slightly breadier cousin of my corn casserole.

So, I built my own corn muffin that behaves exactly the way I want it to. These muffins turn out soft, a little sweet, and clearly corn-forward. The Greek yogurt and extra fat carry the texture, and the whole kernels make sure it still tastes like corn, not just sugar and flour.

## Snapshot

- **Implements:** mixing bowl; whisk; spatula; muffin tin; measuring cups and spoons
- **Oven setting:** 400°F (204°C)
- **Batch size:** 12 muffins

## Ingredients

- 1 box Jiffy corn muffin mix
- 1 cup corn kernels (1 can, drained, or 1 cup frozen, thawed)
- 2 large eggs
- ¾ cup Greek yogurt
- ⅓ to ½ cup milk
- 4 to 6 Tbsp butter, melted
- ½ Tsp black pepper (optional)
- Flaky sea salt, for topping

## Method

1. **Preheat and prep**

   Preheat the oven to 400°F (204°C). Line or grease a 12-cup muffin tin. (I never have liners, I just spray with avocado oil!)

2. **Make the yogurt base**

   In a bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt and milk (I use whole, but you do you) until smooth and pourable. You're aiming for something like thick pancake batter, not stiff and scoopable.

3. **Add eggs and butter**

   Whisk in the eggs and melted butter until fully combined.

4. **Add mix and corn**

   Stir in the Jiffy mix, then fold in most of the corn. (You just fold it in, David!) Reserve a small handful for topping your muffins. Mix just until combined. Overmixing is no bueno. The texture will tighten up.

5. **Adjust texture if needed**

   The batter should be thick but pourable. If it feels dense or stiff, add a splash of milk and stir gently.

6. **Fill and top**

   Divide the batter evenly into the muffin cups. Top with the reserved corn and a light sprinkle of flaky salt.

7. **Bake**

   Bake for 15 to 20 minutes, until the tops are lightly golden and set. You can baste with more melted butter 3 to 5 minutes before you pull the muffins, if you want more butter flavor.

8. **Check doneness**

   Insert a cake tester into the center of a middle muffin. It should come out mostly clean with a few moist crumbs. If you hit a corn kernel, test a different spot.

9. **Cool briefly and serve**

   Let the muffins cool in the pan for about 5 minutes, then remove and serve warm.

## Notes, swaps, and guardrails

### Texture dial

- Use closer to 6 Tbsp butter for a softer, richer muffin
- Use closer to ⅓ cup milk for slightly more structure
- Use closer to ½ cup milk for a looser, more tender crumb

### Corn options

- Canned corn should be well drained so you don't water down the batter
- Frozen corn should be thawed first; no need to dry aggressively

### Flavor push

- Black pepper adds a subtle contrast to the sweetness and is worth including
- A light drizzle of honey right after baking leans more into the sweet side
